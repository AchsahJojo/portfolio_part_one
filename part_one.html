Data Description

  1. Identify your data source.

The data is from the Sonoma County Animal Shelter. https://data.sonomacounty.ca.gov/Government/Animal-Shelter-Intake-and-Outcome/924a-vesw/about_data

  1. Describe your data, including variables and data types.

The dataset includes a mix of categorical, numeric, and date variables. Some of the main variables I expect to use are:

Animal Type: categorical; whether the animal is a dog, cat, or another type

Breed : categorical; the breed or breed mix of the dog

Intake Type : categorical; how the dog entered the shelter, such as stray, owner surrender, or transfer

Intake Condition : categorical; the dog’s condition when entering the shelter

Outcome Type : categorical; the result for the dog, such as adoption, euthanasia, return to owner, or transfer

Sex : categorical; the sex of the dog

Size : categorical; the size category of the dog

Date of Birth – date; estimate age

Intake Date : date; the date the dog entered the shelter

Outcome Date : date; the date the dog left the shelter or received an outcome

Days in Shelter : numeric; the number of days the dog spent in the shelter

  1. Identify the research questions you want to answer.

My primary goal is to analyze how different factors influence a dog’s length of stay in the shelter and likelihood of adoption. By understanding these relationships, I hope to suggest strategies for improving adoption campaigns, resource allocation, and shelter policies.

The main research questions for this project are:

  • How does breed affect a dog’s average length of stay in the shelter?

  • Does intake type affect how long a dog stays in the shelter?

  • Which dog characteristics are associated with faster adoption?

  • Which dog characteristics are associated with a higher likelihood of adoption rather than other outcomes such as euthanasia or transfer?

  • Are there seasonal trends in dog adoptions or shelter stays?

  • Do factors such as size, age, or intake condition appear to influence adoption outcomes?

Data Visualization

  1. What do you want your final visualizations to look like?

I want my final visualizations to be clear and easy to interpret for someone who may not have a technical background. I expect to use a mix of:

  • Bar charts to compare average length of stay across breeds, intake types, or size groups

  • Boxplots to show the spread and variation in shelter stay for different categories

  • Stacked or grouped bar charts to compare outcome types such as adoption, euthanasia, and transfer across groups

  • Line charts to show adoption trends over time or by month

  1. What do you want to highlight on your final visualizations in order to answer your research questions? How do you plan to do that?

I want the visualizations to highlight which groups of dogs stay in the shelter the longest, which groups are adopted the fastest, and whether certain characteristics are associated with more positive or negative outcomes. To find out more about this, I will:

  • Compare average or median days in shelter across breeds, intake types, and size categories

  • Highlight the most common or most important categories rather than showing every breed if there are too many

  • Use outcome comparisons to show whether some groups are more likely to be adopted, euthanized, or transferred

  • Show time based trends to see whether adoptions increase during certain months or seasons
